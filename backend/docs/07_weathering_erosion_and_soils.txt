TOPIC: Weathering, Erosion, and Soils

WEATHERING
Weathering breaks down rock at or near Earth’s surface.

Mechanical weathering
Breaks rock into smaller pieces without changing mineral composition.

Examples:
- Frost wedging
- Root growth
- Salt crystal growth
- Thermal expansion and contraction
- Abrasion
- Pressure release and exfoliation

Chemical weathering
Changes minerals into new substances or dissolves them.

Examples:
- Hydrolysis
- Oxidation
- Dissolution
- Carbonation

FACTORS CONTROLLING WEATHERING
- Climate
- Rock type and mineral composition
- Surface area
- Soil and biological activity
- Time
- Slope and drainage

EROSION
Erosion removes and transports weathered material.

Main agents:
- Running water
- Wind
- Glaciers
- Waves
- Gravity

DEPOSITION
Deposition occurs when transported sediment settles.
- Faster-moving water can carry larger particles.
- When velocity decreases, larger particles settle first.
- Sorting describes how similar sediment grains are in size.

MASS WASTING
Downslope movement caused mainly by gravity.

Types:
- Rockfall
- Slump
- Landslide
- Mudflow
- Debris flow
- Creep

SOIL
Soil is a mixture of mineral particles, organic matter, water, air, and living organisms.

Common soil horizons:
- O horizon: organic material
- A horizon: topsoil
- E horizon: zone of leaching
- B horizon: zone of accumulation
- C horizon: partly weathered parent material
- R horizon: bedrock

KEY DISTINCTION
Weathering breaks material down.
Erosion moves the material.
Deposition drops the material.
