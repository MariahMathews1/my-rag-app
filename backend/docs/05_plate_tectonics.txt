TOPIC: Plate Tectonics

CORE IDEA
Earth’s rigid outer shell is broken into tectonic plates that move over the weaker asthenosphere.

EARTH LAYERS RELEVANT TO PLATE TECTONICS

Lithosphere
- Rigid outer layer
- Includes the crust and uppermost mantle
- Broken into tectonic plates

Asthenosphere
- Weak, ductile part of the upper mantle beneath the lithosphere
- Flows slowly and allows plates to move

PLATE-MOTION DRIVERS
- Slab pull: a dense oceanic slab sinks and pulls the rest of the plate
- Ridge push: elevated oceanic lithosphere moves away from a mid-ocean ridge
- Mantle convection: heat-driven circulation contributes to plate motion

DIVERGENT BOUNDARIES
Plates move apart.

Oceanic divergence:
- Forms mid-ocean ridges
- Magma rises and creates new oceanic crust
- Produces shallow earthquakes and basaltic volcanism

Continental divergence:
- Stretches and thins continental crust
- Creates rift valleys
- May eventually open a new ocean basin

CONVERGENT BOUNDARIES
Plates move toward one another.

Oceanic-continental convergence:
- Denser oceanic plate subducts
- Produces a trench and continental volcanic arc
- Generates earthquakes from shallow to deep

Oceanic-oceanic convergence:
- One oceanic plate subducts
- Produces a trench and volcanic island arc

Continental-continental convergence:
- Neither continent subducts easily
- Crust thickens and forms large mountain belts
- Produces strong earthquakes but limited volcanism

TRANSFORM BOUNDARIES
- Plates slide horizontally past one another
- Crust is neither created nor destroyed
- Earthquakes are common and usually shallow
- Example: San Andreas Fault

HOT SPOTS
A hot spot is a long-lived source of magma beneath a plate.
- A moving plate can create a chain of volcanoes
- Volcano ages generally increase away from the active hot spot

SEAFLOOR SPREADING
- New oceanic crust forms at mid-ocean ridges
- Magnetic stripes on both sides of a ridge record reversals in Earth’s magnetic field
- Ocean crust becomes older away from the ridge

PLATE-TECTONIC EVIDENCE
- Matching continental coastlines
- Fossil correlations across oceans
- Matching rock units and mountain belts
- Paleoclimate evidence
- Global earthquake and volcano patterns
- Seafloor magnetic stripes
- Age pattern of oceanic crust

KEY LANDFORMS
- Mid-ocean ridge
- Rift valley
- Deep-ocean trench
- Volcanic arc
- Island arc
- Folded mountain belt
- Transform fault
