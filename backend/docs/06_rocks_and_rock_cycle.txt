TOPIC: Rocks and the Rock Cycle

THREE MAIN ROCK GROUPS
- Igneous
- Sedimentary
- Metamorphic

IGNEOUS ROCKS
Igneous rocks form when molten rock cools and solidifies.

Intrusive igneous rocks
- Cool slowly below the surface
- Usually have large visible crystals
- Example: granite

Extrusive igneous rocks
- Cool quickly at or near the surface
- Usually have small crystals or glassy texture
- Example: basalt

Common igneous textures:
- Coarse-grained
- Fine-grained
- Porphyritic
- Glassy
- Vesicular
- Pyroclastic

SEDIMENTARY ROCKS
Sedimentary rocks form from accumulated sediment, chemical precipitation, or biological material.

Clastic sedimentary rocks
- Made from fragments of older rocks
- Classified largely by grain size
- Examples: conglomerate, sandstone, shale

Chemical sedimentary rocks
- Form when dissolved minerals precipitate
- Examples: rock salt and some limestone

Biochemical or organic sedimentary rocks
- Form from remains of organisms
- Examples: fossiliferous limestone and coal

Lithification:
- Compaction presses sediment together
- Cementation binds grains with precipitated minerals

METAMORPHIC ROCKS
Metamorphic rocks form when preexisting rock is changed by heat, pressure, and chemically active fluids without completely melting.

Foliated metamorphic rocks
- Minerals align in layers or bands
- Common sequence with increasing metamorphic grade:
  shale → slate → phyllite → schist → gneiss

Nonfoliated metamorphic rocks
- Lack a layered texture
- Marble forms from limestone
- Quartzite forms from quartz sandstone

METAMORPHISM TYPES
Contact metamorphism
- Caused mainly by heat near an igneous intrusion

Regional metamorphism
- Occurs over large areas during mountain building
- Involves strong heat and pressure

ROCK CYCLE
Any rock type can change into another through geologic processes.

Examples:
- Weathering and erosion produce sediment.
- Burial, compaction, and cementation create sedimentary rock.
- Heat and pressure create metamorphic rock.
- Melting creates magma.
- Cooling and crystallization create igneous rock.
- Uplift exposes buried rocks to weathering.

KEY IDEA
The rock cycle is not a single circular path. Rocks can follow many different pathways depending on geologic conditions.
